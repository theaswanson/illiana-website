December 4-Christmas Party at the Bodines.

 

WOW!  WHAT A FUN DAY!

 

Weren’t we blessed with some beautiful weather?!  We couldn’t have asked for a nicer day.

The hog was done to perfection......THANKS DEAN AND MONIQUE.  Speaking of the hog...a little tidbit about what goes on “behind the scenes”.  Fontaines volunteered to pick up the hog on Saturday.  It was scheduled for 7:30 P.M. pickup.  Donna and Jane Reavley drove off to meet Roy and Linda Smith (the owners of Wallace Processing).  When they got there, they waited, and waited, and waited.  No one showed up!  Seems the Smiths forgot all about the meeting and were shopping at Walmart in Danville, Il.  Luckily, the town of Wallace was having an Octoberfest celebration, so Donna and Jane were entertained while they waited!  Needless to say, it was quite late when they pulled in to the show grounds with the hog.

-4:30 A.M.-SUNDAY

The roar of Dean’s motorcycle woke up Terry and Don Bodine (who were camping at the show grounds along with Pat and Ed Grubb).  It was time to start cooking the hog.  To pass the time, Dean brought along his guitar  So , Terry cranked up the old Minneapolis R, hooked up the old water wagon, and headed down to the Grubb’s camper.  Once there, Dean serenaded Ed and Pat with some favorite tunes.  Ed said he woke up thinking he was in Mexico!  So, as you can see, a good time (and laughs) were had even before anyone showed up for the day!

Newest member Jim Lawhorn, picked up the apples from  Coffing Bros.  Orchard .  THANKS JIM!   Plenty of good cider was enjoyed.  Hope everyone got to take some home with them.  THANKS BODINE’S for bringing the cider press!

-2-

 

Don Bodine had his steam engine fired up.  Pete von Stein took his tractor and plowed the west field.  Dick Fontaine, Charlie Lupinek, Cliff Hadlock, Joe Kingsbury, and Nathan Bryant all had their trains running.  There were plenty of passengers waiting for a ride.  Steve Dye brought along the engine he’s been working on.  Will it be ready by next year?

Our talented Kathy Olin and her family brought along some old fashion cooking, spinning, and sewing.  Denny made some cracklings, Kathy’s dad chopped cabbage for some slaw (which was delicious) and they made some tasty potatoes for the dinner.  Jim Manlief had his interesting display of miniature engines “puttering and sputtering”.

At 2:00 P.M. everyone lined up to enjoy a table of food and, WHAT A SPREAD!  (Think we out-did ourselves this time!)  With such delicious dishes, our next cook book should be better than the first one.  SEND ANNE THOSE RECIPES!

What an enjoyable day we had.  No one could have gone home hungry!  Let’s all take a moment and give thanks for good food, good weather, and good fellowship!  THANKS FOR ALL WHO CAME AND HELPED!!
