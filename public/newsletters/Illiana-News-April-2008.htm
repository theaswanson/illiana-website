INDIANA POEM:

It’s winter in Indiana and the gentle breezes blow

Seventy miles an hour at twenty-five below.

Oh, how I love Indiana when the snow’s up to your butt

You take a breath of winter and your nose gets frozen shut.

Yes, the weather here is wonderful so I guess I’ll hang around

I could never leave Indiana ‘cause I’m frozen to the ground!

Submitted by: Rolland McKinney

 

MEETING:

The above poem was so cute I couldn’t resist including it in this newsletter even though the spring flowers are “poking through the ground”.  Hopefully we’ll have some nice weather from now on!  The next meeting of the Illiana Antique Power Assoc.  will be April 21, 2008 at the club house.  Dinner will begin at 6:30 P.M. so bring a dish to pass......our dinners seem to get better and better!  Attend the meeting and see our new cabinets installed through-out the club house.  They look fabulous!  SEE YOU THERE!

 

WORK DAYS:

Several members attended the April 3rd work day and got a good start on “Kathy’s Kitchen”.  Kathy Olin served dinner to the hard workers with Joan Fry supplying a yummy desert.  The next work day is scheduled for April 10th.  It’s exciting to have a permanent building for the ladies to show off their old fashion house-keeping talents.  More exciting news is that the blacksmiths will also have a new permanent building started soon and ready for this year’s show!

 

RAFFLE TICKETS:
